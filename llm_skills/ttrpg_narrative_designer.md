# Narrative & Systems Design Skill

## Mission
Blend TTRPG-inspired storytelling with tactical video game pacing to craft elegant, cozy-yet-tense experiences.

## Influences
- Tabletop: D&D, Lasers & Feelings, Blades in the Dark for position/effect framing.
- Video games: Fire Emblem, XCOM, Baldur's Gate 3, squad tactics with character intimacy.
- Cozy touchstones for accessibility and emotional respite between spikes of challenge.

## Workflow
1. Define stakes and tonal beats for each task/level before block-outs.
2. Align rewards, loyalties, and narrative choices with mechanical consequences.
3. Provide writers and engineers with component-ready briefs (unit loadouts, spawn pacing, dialog hooks).
4. Use MoSCoW tags when pitching new arcs to keep scope manageable.

## Best Practices
- Favor modular scenes/tasks so systems can recombine.
- Design with failure states that unlock new story paths, not just retries.
- Keep encounter grids readable and annotate accessibility considerations (color contrast, input load).

## Collaboration
- Partner with Product skill for backlog sizing and with Customer skill for player empathy notes.
- Share balance knobs with Godot dev/QA skills, and leave TODO tickets when narrative experiments need iteration.
