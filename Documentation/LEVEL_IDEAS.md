# Level Ideas and Tutorial Concepts

This document outlines potential level designs, starting with foundational tutorials and expanding into complex mechanical scenarios.

## Tutorial Levels

These levels are designed to introduce players to core game mechanics in a controlled environment.

### 1. Terrain Tutorial: "The High Ground"
*   **Focus**: Introduction to different tile types and their effects on movement and combat.
*   **Mechanics**:
	*   **Forest**: Provides cover but slows movement.
	*   **Mountains**: High defense bonus, blocking line of sight.
	*   **Swamps**: High movement cost, potential status debuffs.
*   **Objective**: Reach the objective by navigating through varying terrain, using cover to avoid scripted archer fire.

### 2. Weather Tutorial: "Eyes in the Storm"
*   **Focus**: Dynamic weather effects and their impact on visibility and stats.
*   **Mechanics**:
	*   **Fog**: Reduces vision range.
	*   **Rain**: Penalizes fire-based abilities and reduces accuracy.
	*   **Storm**: Lowers morale and can randomly strike tiles with lightning.
*   **Objective**: Escort a messenger through a changing weather front, timing movements to avoid the worst of the storm.

### 3. Neutrals Tutorial: "Diplomatic Ties"
*   **Focus**: Interacting with non-hostile units and using the Morale/Dialogue system.
*   **Mechanics**:
	*   **Recruitment**: Using dialogue to bring neutrals to your side.
	*   **Information Gathering**: Talking to villagers to reveal hidden map locations.
	*   **Bribing/Trading**: Using resources to bypass neutral blockades.
*   **Objective**: Secure passage through a neutral village without starting a combat encounter.

### 4. Enemies Tutorial: "Baptism by Fire"
*   **Focus**: Basic combat, unit types, and enemy AI behavior.
*   **Mechanics**:
	*   **Unit Roles**: Melee vs. Ranged vs. Support.
	*   **Zone of Control**: Preventing enemies from moving past your front line.
	*   **Flanking**: Gaining bonuses by attacking from multiple sides.
*   **Objective**: Clear a small bandit camp using a balanced squad.

### 5. Items Tutorial: "Relics of the Past"
*   **Focus**: Finding, equipping, and using consumables and equipment.
*   **Mechanics**:
	*   **Looting**: Searching ruins or chests.
	*   **Inventory Management**: Swapping items between units.
	*   **Active Items**: Using potions or scrolls during a turn.
*   **Objective**: Recover a stolen artifact from a tomb by using specialized items found along the way.

### 6. Locations Tutorial: "Strategic Points"
*   **Focus**: Capturing and holding key map structures.
*   **Mechanics**:
	*   **Watchtowers**: Granting massive vision range.
	*   **Barracks**: Spawning reinforcements or healing units.
	*   **Shrines**: Providing temporary global buffs.
*   **Objective**: Capture and hold three strategic points for 5 turns to signal for reinforcements.

## Concept Expansions

Once players understand the basics, these concepts can be expanded into high-stakes scenarios.

### 1. The Colossus (Enemy Expansion)
*   **Concept**: One huge boss enemy occupying multiple hexes.
*   **Mechanics**: The boss has different health pools for different body parts (e.g., Legs, Arms, Head). Players must destroy the legs to lower its defense or the arms to stop its "Sweep" attack.

### 2. Shifting Allegiances (Neutrals Expansion)
*   **Concept**: Complex loyalty system with neutral factions.
*   **Mechanics**: A three-way battle where neutrals might join you or your enemy based on your actions mid-level (e.g., if you accidentally damage their property, they turn hostile; if you save their leader, they join your side).

### 3. The Cursed Hoard (Items Expansion)
*   **Concept**: Many trapped or "monkey's paw" items.
*   **Mechanics**: The map is littered with powerful items, but many are trapped or cursed. Players must use "Identify" skills or take risks. Picking up a "Trapped" item might trigger a local explosion or spawn hidden enemies.

### 4. Divided Front (Locations Expansion)
*   **Concept**: Defend 2 out of 3 locations.
*   **Mechanics**: The player is forced to split their forces to defend three separate villages. Losing one is acceptable, but losing two results in failure. This forces difficult decisions about resource allocation and unit positioning.
