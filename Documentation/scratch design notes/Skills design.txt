Basic
Grit — “Smash”

A direct, forceful blow delivered without finesse.

High physical damage

Minor morale damage through intimidation

Reliable, hard to mitigate

Reads immediately as brute force

Player intuition: hit them until they break

Flow — “Slip”

A precise strike delivered while repositioning.

Moderate damage

Small morale damage from disorientation

Ignores some positional penalties

Emphasizes motion and timing

Player intuition: hit and move

Gusto — “Press”

An assertive advance that overwhelms resolve.

Moderate damage

Strong morale pressure

Gains bonus if acting first or after movement

Feels aggressive, not heavy

Player intuition: keep the pressure on

Temper — “Check”

A controlled, corrective strike meant to halt action.

Lower raw damage

Significant morale suppression

Slightly reduces enemy follow-up effectiveness

Reads as cold, measured force

Player intuition: stop them from doing what they want

Clarity — “Mark”

A precise hit that exposes weakness.

Light immediate damage

Sets up increased damage or morale loss later

Strong informational feedback

Clean mental framing

Player intuition: this makes the next hit matter more

Shine — “Rally”

A bold strike that lifts allies and unnerves the foe.

Light to moderate damage

Morale damage to target

Small morale boost to nearby allies

Social and visible

Player intuition: win the moment, not just the hit

Why these work together

All are single-target, nearby, usable every turn

Damage ranges differ, but none are traps

Morale effects are distinct in cause (fear, confusion, pressure, doubt)

Labels are short, readable, and expandable into advanced variants

Variety
Below are **concrete, legible ways characters can attack one another using each attribute**, framed so they feel **distinct in method, pacing, and counterplay**, not just damage flavor. Each list emphasizes *how* the attack pressures the opponent rather than a cosmetic effect.

---

## **Gusto** (wind, drive, outward force)

Attacks that overwhelm by momentum, presence, or initiative.

* Sudden charge that forces the opponent to react off-balance
* Verbal or physical feint that draws an early response, then punishes it
* Pressuring advance that shrinks safe movement space
* Rapid reposition followed by a strike from an unexpected angle
* Intimidating shout or display that disrupts timing
* Forcing the opponent into terrain they did not choose
* Chain actions that deny recovery windows
* Attacks that grow stronger if the attacker keeps moving or acting

**Counterplay:** patience, bracing, space denial

---

## **Temper** (cold, restraint, resistance)

Attacks that deny, exhaust, or blunt the opponent’s intent.

* Parry that converts the opponent’s force into self-damage or delay
* Chilling stare or presence that slows reactions
* Locking an opponent into a defensive posture they cannot exit cleanly
* Absorbing a hit to negate follow-ups
* Counterstrike that only triggers if the enemy overcommits
* Sustained pressure that drains stamina or morale rather than health
* Interrupts that cancel charged or multi-step actions
* Turning enemy aggression into predictability

**Counterplay:** unpredictability, patience breaks, sustained pressure

---

## **Clarity** (insight, perception, precision)

Attacks that exploit knowledge, timing, or structure.

* Targeting a known weakness or exposed joint
* Predictive strike based on the opponent’s previous patterns
* Marking an enemy so later hits land harder
* Disabling a tool, limb, or resource rather than dealing raw damage
* Forcing a choice between two bad options
* Delayed attack set up several turns earlier
* Illusions or misdirection that collapse once the truth is revealed
* Exploiting terrain geometry perfectly

**Counterplay:** randomness, emotional play, obscuring information

---

## **Grit** (mass, endurance, inevitability)

Attacks that succeed because they cannot be stopped.

* Heavy blow that ignores partial defenses
* Advancing strike that moves both characters
* Attacks that get stronger as the fight drags on
* Crushing grapple or pin
* Environmental damage caused by sheer force
* Trading blows favorably instead of avoiding them
* Breaking cover, shields, or terrain
* Retaliation damage when struck

**Counterplay:** mobility, attrition avoidance, precision

---

## **Flow** (adaptability, redirection, continuity)

Attacks that turn movement, positioning, and change into advantage.

* Redirecting an enemy’s strike into another target
* Slipping past defenses to strike from within reach
* Attacks that change type mid-execution
* Using terrain as a moving weapon
* Strikes that reposition both attacker and defender
* Evading while dealing damage simultaneously
* Chaining actions across allies and enemies
* Turning environmental hazards against the opponent

**Counterplay:** anchors, traps, fixed zones, prediction

---

## **Shine** (warmth, morale, influence)

Attacks that act through emotion, confidence, or social force.

* Bolstering allies so the enemy is outnumbered psychologically
* Forcing hesitation through compassion, doubt, or awe
* Drawing attacks toward oneself to protect others
* Inspiring a surge that breaks a stalemate
* Turning a crowd or neutral units against the enemy
* Attacks that grow stronger with witnesses
* Demoralizing an opponent through visible resolve
* Winning a conflict without finishing the fight

**Counterplay:** isolation, cynicism, suppression

---

## **Why this holds together**

* No attribute is just “damage with flavor”
* Each attacks **a different layer**: timing, stamina, space, certainty, momentum, or morale
* Characters can mix attributes without contradiction
* Counters are **behavioral**, not rock-paper-scissors
