# HEX: Player Quickstart Guide

Welcome to HEX! This guide covers the basics of movement, combat, and surviving the shifting environment.

## 1. The Hex Grid and Distance
HEX is played on a hexagonal grid. Movement and attack ranges are measured in "hexes" (tiles).
- **Movement Points (M)**: Each tile costs a certain amount of Movement Points to enter. Rough terrain or hazards cost more.
- **Distance**: The distance to a target is the number of hexes you must cross to reach them. Melee attacks typically require you to be near (distance of 1).

## 2. Action Points and Command Costs
During a turn, your units have **Movement Points** and **Action Points**.
- **Move (M)**: Uses Movement Points. You can move until your Movement Points run out or you perform a turn-ending Action.
- **Action (A)**: Most significant commands (Attacking, Convincing, Looting, Exploring) cost an Action Point (usually denoted as **A1** in the UI).
- **Turn Flow**: Performing an Action generally ends that unit's turn, even if they have Movement Points left. Plan your positioning *before* you act!

## 3. The Six-Stat Model
Units use three pairs of attributes for interactions and combat:
- **Body**: Grit & Flow (Physical endurance and agility)
- **Mind**: Gusto & Focus (Assertive push and careful restraint)
- **Spirit**: Shine & Shade (Inspiration and perception)

When you attack or interact, you will use the relevant stat. For example, "Convincing" someone might use *Shine*, while forcing open a gate might use *Grit*.

## 4. Morale and Willpower
- **Willpower (WP)** is your unit's health. If WP reaches 0, the unit is defeated.
- **Morale** is the collective Willpower of your entire faction. If your faction's total Willpower drops too low, your forces will break and retreat, failing the mission. Protect your units to keep the Morale Advantage Bar in your favor!

## 5. Weather and Pressures
The weather in HEX changes and affects gameplay (e.g., *Storm Winds* might reduce visibility or movement).
- **Forecasts**: Pay attention to the Weather Panel. If a new pressure is forecasted, it will apply on the *next* round. Use this warning to prepare!
- **Channeling**: Some units can channel a new weather pressure, altering the battlefield conditions to your advantage.
